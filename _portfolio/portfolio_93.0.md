title: "Phenotypic comparison of high-altitude African accessions of *Arabidopsis thaliana*"
excerpt: "African populations of *Arabidopsis thaliana* harbor high levels of genetic variation, providing a powerful system for studying local adaptation. By comparing *A. thaliana* accessions collected from different alpine mountain environments, this work investigates patterns of parallel evolution in alpine-adaptive traits and links phenotypic divergence to underlying genetic variation across environmental gradients."

### Aims
- Phenotypic traits are correlated with high-altitude adaptation  
- Photoperiod effects influence plant phenotypic variation across accessions  
- Identification of optimal conditions to promote seed germination  
- Determination of minimal vernalization duration required to induce flowering
