<img src="/images/HK_tree.png" alt="Weixuan Ning" style="float:right; width:200px; margin: 0 0 1em 1em; border-radius:10px;"> I am **Weixuan Ning**, a plant evolutionary biologist focused on understanding how plants evolve across both natural and human-influenced systems. My research centers on the use of **<u>genomic data</u>** to uncover patterns of variation, adaptation, and divergence, with particular interest in processes such as **<u>conservation</u>** and **<u>domestication</u>**. By integrating population genomics, evolutionary theory, and comparative approaches, I aim to reveal how genetic diversity is shaped over time and how these patterns inform both the history and future resilience of plant species.

A key direction of my work is exploring complex evolutionary mechanisms, especially **<u>polyploidization</u>**, and its role in shaping plant genomes and phenotypes. I am particularly interested in how genome duplication influences adaptation, trait evolution, and the emergence of novel diversity, especially in **<u>crop species</u>** and their **<u>wild relatives</u>**.

Through this lens, my research bridges fundamental evolutionary questions with applied challenges in **<u>agriculture</u>** and **<u>biodiversity conservation</u>**, contributing to a deeper understanding of plant evolution in a rapidly changing world.
