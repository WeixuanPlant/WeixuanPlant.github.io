I am **Weixuan Ning**, a plant evolutionary biologist focused on understanding how plants evolve across both natural and human-influenced systems. My research centers on the use of genomic data to uncover patterns of variation, adaptation, and divergence, with particular interest in processes such as **genomic conservation** and **crop domestication**. <img src="/images/me.png" alt="Weixuan Ning" style="float:right; width:400px; margin: 0.5em 0.5em 0.5em 0.5em; border-radius:10px;"> By integrating population genomics, evolutionary theory, and comparative approaches, I aim to reveal how genetic diversity is shaped over time and how these patterns inform both the history and future resilience of plant species. 

A key direction of my work is exploring complex evolutionary mechanisms, especially **polyploidization**, and its role in shaping plant genomes and phenotypes. I am particularly interested in how genome duplication influences adaptation, trait evolution, and the emergence of novel diversity, especially in crop species and their **wild relatives**.

Through this lens, my research bridges fundamental evolutionary questions with applied challenges in agriculture and biodiversity conservation, contributing to a deeper understanding of plant evolution in a rapidly changing world.
